Forklift

A forklift, also known as a lift truck or forklift truck, is a versatile industrial vehicle designed for lifting, transporting, and stacking heavy loads and materials within warehouses, manufacturing facilities, distribution centers, and construction sites. Featuring two fork-like prongs or specialized attachments, a forklift is capable of raising and lowering goods, facilitating efficient material handling. These vehicles come in various sizes, including compact electric models for indoor use and larger, robust machines powered by gasoline, diesel, or propane engines for outdoor and heavy-duty applications. Operated from a driver's seat, forklifts are equipped with controls to manipulate the forks, steer, and navigate in confined spaces, making them indispensable tools for streamlining logistics, improving productivity, and enhancing safety in a wide range of industrial and commercial environments.
